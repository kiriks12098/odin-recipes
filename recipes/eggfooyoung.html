Egg Foo Young

Description

Egg foo youg is a Chinese American dish similar to an omelette. The dish consists primarily of egg, bean sprouts, and onion with an optional meat added. The omlette is fried in oil and then is usually topped with a brown gravy.

It is common for some Chinese restaurants to serve the brown gravy on the side, but I've always prefered to have the omelette resting in a dish containing the gravy.

Ingredients

    OMELETTE

  • 6 eggs
  • 2 cups bean sprouts
  • 4 shallots/green onions
  • Salt
  • White pepper
  • 1 minced garlic clove
  • Optional meat filling
  • Neutral flavored oil

    • SAUCE

  • 4 tsp corn starch
  • 1 1/2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp mirin
  • 1/2 tsp sesame oil
  • 250 ml water
  • White pepper

Steps

    Sauce:

  1. Mix corn starch and soy sauce, then add the remaining ingredients.
  2. Pour ingredients into a sauce pan over medium heat. Bring to a simmer, stirring constantly. Simmer for one minute until sauce thickens to a thin syrup consistency. Remove from heat and set aside.

    Omelette:

  1. Whisk eggs in a bowl.
  2. Add bean sprouts, shallots/onions, optional meat, salt and pepper. Mix until incorporated.
  3. Heat 1/2 tbsp of oil in a nonstick skillet or wok. Add some garlic and saute for 10 seconds, then push into the center of the pan.
  4. Ladle in 1/4 of the egg mixture. Push the ingredients into a round shape.
  5. Cook until the underside is a light golden color (1-2 minutes), then flip and cook the other side for 1 minute.
  6. Slide omelette onto a plate. Pour sauce over the omlette and add green onions or sesame seeds if desired.
  7. Repeat for the reamaining egg mixture.
